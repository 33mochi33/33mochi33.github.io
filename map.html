Yellowstone National Park, located primarily in the U.S. state of Wyoming but also extending into Montana and Idaho, stands as one of the world's most iconic and oldest national parks. Established in 1872, it holds the distinction of being not only the first national park in the United States but also the first anywhere in the world. Yellowstone is renowned for its extraordinary geothermal features, including geysers, hot springs, and mud pots, with the most famous being Old Faithful, a geyser known for its predictable eruptions. The park's diverse ecosystems support a rich array of wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, bison, and elk. Its expansive wilderness encompasses dramatic canyons, alpine rivers, lush forests, and the vast Yellowstone Lake, offering visitors unparalleled opportunities for outdoor recreation, wildlife viewing, and awe-inspiring natural beauty.
